Why this and not generative

Most spatial AI work right now is generative. Image to 3D, prompt to scene, video to world. That's a fine direction for content. It's the wrong direction for buildings and streets, where the consequence of a hallucinated detail is somebody losing money or somebody getting hurt. Polymetron goes the other way. The capture is real, the hotspots point to ordinances and census tracts you can look up yourself, and nothing in the reasoning chain is generated.

We've been running a food-system mapping platform with Georgia Tech and the City of Atlanta for the past year. The team and the data pipeline are the same. Buildings and streets are what we're adding now.
